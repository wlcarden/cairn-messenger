# §§6/7 — Pilot-to-Broader-Release Gates Lens

## Summary

§§6 and 7 frame the v1 → v1.5 → v2 → v3 trajectory as a sequence of release-scope expansions whose dependencies are _architectural and engineering_ (forward-compatibility schemas, capability-token extension points, multi-device protocol slots). The release-gating commitments that §§8.5, 8.4, and 10 establish — that broader-than-pilot release is conditional on the **pre-beta audit closing** (§8.5, D0011), conditional on **Phase C funding** materializing (§10.3), and that the structural mitigations users would receive in broader release (formal Safe Harbor per D0012, board-bound governance per §8.4, formal partner advisory authority per D0009) are _gated on foundation incorporation_ (~18–24 months post-v1, D0010) — are absent from §§6 and 7 entirely. §6.3 closes pilot framing on a "six months of active use **before any broader release decision**" (line 634) phrasing that elides the audit and funding gates §10.3 makes structural; §7.1 v1.5 is described as a "~6–9 months post-v1" release with no acknowledgment that the gap between v1 pilot and v1.5 release contains a pre-beta audit (§8.5), a foundation-incorporation work stream (§8.4, ~18–24 months), and a Phase C funding event (§10.3, $105K floor) that may not close.

The result is a §§6/7 surface that reads as a release roadmap when it is actually a _conditional-release_ roadmap, and the conditions are documented in three different sections (§8.4, §8.5, §10) that a §§6/7-first reader (funder skimming the v1 scope, partner reviewing the roadmap, pilot user evaluating what they receive) will not have read first. The gap is not in the brief's substance — §8.5 line 813 and §10.3 line 1098 both name the no-skip-the-audit posture explicitly — but in §§6/7's failure to reference these gates at the point readers most need them.

## Critical findings

### F1: §6.3 frames "broader release decision" as conditional only on pilot completion, eliding the audit gate and Phase C funding gate

- **Evidence:** §6.3 line 634: _"Six months of active use before any broader release decision."_ Line 634's continuation describes pilot duration in terms of what observation it enables ("observations from the early pilot can inform v1.5 design") without naming what other conditions must close before broader-than-pilot release. The pre-beta audit is the structural gate per §8.5 line 813 ("the project does not ship broader-than-pilot release without a pre-beta audit") and per D0011. Phase C funding is the resourcing gate per §10.3 line 1096–1098 ("What Phase C unlocks. Broader-than-pilot release… If Phase C funding does not close at all, the pilot continues at the Phase B-funded posture and broader release is deferred indefinitely"). Neither is referenced in §6.3.
- **Impact:** A funder, partner, or pilot user reading §6.3 in isolation acquires the model "pilot completes at 6 months → broader release decision happens." The actual model per §8.5 and §10.3 is "pilot completes → pre-beta audit must close → Phase C funding must close → foundation incorporation work must be in progress → _then_ broader release." A pilot user who consented to v1 on the §6.3 framing would not know they may be operating at pilot scale indefinitely under the §10.3 contingency. A funder evaluating Phase B against the §6.3 framing may treat Phase B as funding the path to broader release rather than as funding the path to a pilot that is itself gated to broader release by a separate (Phase C) event.
- **Recommendation:** Add to §6.3 (after "Pilot duration" or as a new "Pilot exit conditions" paragraph) the explicit gating language: pilot-to-broader-release is gated on (1) pre-beta audit closing per §8.5 / D0011, (2) Phase C funding closing per §10.3, and (3) foundation-incorporation work materially advancing such that the structural mitigations §10.3 line 1096 enumerates can be operative at broader-release time. Cross-reference §8.5, §10.3, D0011 from §6.3 directly, not only in passing.

### F2: §7.1 release sequence describes v1.5 as a timeline release ("~6–9 months post-v1") without naming that the v1→v1.5 interval contains the broader-release gate

- **Evidence:** §7.1 line 670: _"v1.5 (target ~6-9 months post-v1; the 'architecture-completeness' release)."_ The release sequence treats v1.5 as a development-scope release defined by Briar integration, reproducible builds, and reviewer-attestation transition. It does not name that _broader-than-pilot release happens at or after v1.5_, nor that the v1 pilot → v1.5 release transition is the moment §8.5's no-skip-the-audit commitment, §10.3's Phase C funding event, and §8.4's foundation-incorporation timeline (~18–24 months post-v1 per D0010) all converge. §7.2 "Dependencies between releases" line 682 enumerates only architectural dependencies ("Briar integration in v1.5 reuses the v1 identity model…"). It does not list the funding, audit, and governance dependencies that §§8.4, 8.5, 10 establish as hard gates.
- **Impact:** Readers reading §7 in isolation acquire a model where v1.5 follows v1 on an engineering timeline. A funder asks "what's the path from v1 to v1.5?" and §7 answers in code, schema, and protocol terms — when the §10.3 answer is "Phase C closes" and the §8.5 answer is "pre-beta audit closes." This contradicts §10.3 line 1098 ("broader release is gated on the pre-beta audit closing") at the framing level even though §10.3 itself is internally consistent. A partner evaluating whether to commit to v1.5 facilitation work on the §7.1 timeline does not see that the timeline depends on funding outcomes that §10.3 explicitly says the project does not control.
- **Recommendation:** In §7.1 v1.5, replace "target ~6–9 months post-v1" with timing conditional language that names the gates: "targeting ~6–9 months post-v1 conditional on Phase C funding closing per §10.3 and the pre-beta audit closing per §8.5 / D0011." In §7.2, add a "Non-architectural dependencies" subsection (or expand "What requires architectural revisitation") naming the audit-gate, funding-gate, and incorporation-gate dependencies. The current §7.2 framing treats only architecture as gating; the brief elsewhere makes clear it is not.

### F3: §7 roadmap nowhere references foundation incorporation as the gate for structural mitigations broader-release users receive

- **Evidence:** §10.3 line 1096 enumerates what Phase C unlocks: _"formalized Safe Harbor per D0012, board-bound governance per §8.4, formal partner advisory authority per D0009, and the reviewer-honoraria operating model per §8.2."_ §8.4 line 766 states foundation incorporation is _"approximately 18-24 months post-v1 launch."_ §7 release sequence covers v1.5 (~6–9 months), v1.6 (~12–15 months), v2 (~12–18 months), v3 (~18–24 months) without referencing that v2 and v3 timeline overlaps with foundation-incorporation timing and that the structural protections users receive at broader release depend on incorporation. §7.1 v4 line 676 mentions "established-organization tier" but frames it as a product-tier expansion, not as the foundation-governance milestone §8.4 makes it.
- **Impact:** A reader following §7's release sequence forms a model where v1.5 / v2 / v3 are released to a continuously growing audience under the same governance posture v1 has — a natural-person-operated project. The actual model per §8.4, §10.3, and D0012 is that users receiving broader-than-pilot releases at or near v1.5 onward will receive a _materially different_ protection posture (formalized Safe Harbor, board-bound governance) than v1 pilot users — but only if foundation incorporation closes, which per §10.3 depends on Phase C funding, which per §10.7 line 1160 may not close. Pilot users evaluating v1 consent on §6.3 grounds, and broader-release users evaluating their protections on §7 grounds, are reading materially different protection regimes that §§6 and 7 do not surface.
- **Recommendation:** Add to §7 (either as a new §7.4 "Governance and assurance milestones" or as additions to §7.2 "Dependencies between releases") explicit cross-references to §8.4 foundation incorporation and §10.3 Phase C, naming which roadmap items shift from "stated intent" to "structurally enforceable" at incorporation. The brief's honesty posture in §8.4 line 766 and §10.3 line 1096 is not surfaced where the roadmap-reader would benefit from it.

## Significant findings

### F4: §6.1 v1 scope characterizes the pilot deliverable without naming that pilot users receive a different audit posture than broader-release users will

- **Evidence:** §6.1 line 579 _"Release security. Two-layer signing per Section 5.5… in v1 reviewers review source rather than verify binary equivalence."_ §6.1 does not reference D0011's pre-pilot cryptographic-primitives audit ($15–30K subsidized, per §8.5 line 808) as a v1 deliverable. The audit is named in §8.5 and §10.2 as a Phase B gate to pilot deployment. §6.1's "What ships in v1" surface — the natural place a pilot user would look to understand what they are receiving — does not enumerate the pre-pilot audit as part of what ships.
- **Impact:** The asymmetry between pilot users (who receive a pre-pilot primitives-only audit) and broader-release users (who receive the pre-pilot audit plus the pre-beta full audit) is the entire structural rationale for D0011's two-stage approach. §6.1 elides the pilot-side of that structure. A pilot user reading §6.1 to evaluate what assurance the implementation carries does not see the audit named.
- **Recommendation:** Add to §6.1 a "Pre-pilot assurance" item naming the D0011 pre-pilot primitives-only audit, cross-referencing §8.5 line 808 and D0011. This is a Phase-B-funded deliverable that §6.1 currently treats as out-of-scope-for-§6 when it is in fact the gate to v1 pilot deployment.

### F5: §6.3 "Budget and funding posture" frames Phase B funding as supplementary when §10.2 frames it as gating

- **Evidence:** §6.3 line 638: _"the developer covers pilot hardware out of pocket through v1 launch; partner contributions and grant funding when secured supplement rather than fund the pilot baseline. The pilot does not depend on external funding to proceed."_ §10.2 line 1077 and §10.7 line 1155 establish that Phase B funding (which includes the pre-pilot audit per §10.2 line 1063) is _gating to pilot deployment_, not supplementary: _"The project does not deploy a pilot without the pre-pilot audit, per D0011's no-skip-the-audit posture."_
- **Impact:** §6.3 line 638's "pilot does not depend on external funding to proceed" framing is true at the _hardware_ level (BYOD pilot model per §10.3 line 1089) but false at the _audit_ level. A reader of §6.3 acquires the model that pilot proceeds on developer self-funding; §10.2 / §10.7 establish that pilot is gated on at least Phase B subsidy-program funding closing. The framings are not reconciled.
- **Recommendation:** Revise §6.3 line 638 to distinguish what pilot-baseline self-funding covers (hardware, developer time per Phase A) from what gates pilot deployment (the pre-pilot audit per Phase B, conditional on subsidy-program funding closing). The honest phrasing parallels §10.2 line 1077 directly.

### F6: §7.1 v4 framing treats "established-organization tier" as a product expansion, eliding that it is the funded operational posture §8.1 and §10.4 describe

- **Evidence:** §7.1 line 676: _"v4 and beyond (longer-term horizon). Established-organization tier with formal admin and governance controls, intended as the subsidy mechanism for the grassroots use case the rest of the roadmap supports."_ §10.4 Phase D line 1117 names that maintainer compensation is aspirational and not committed. §10.7 line 1160 names that foundation incorporation funding may not close and the structural mitigations remain at stated-intent posture in that case. §7.1 v4's "subsidy mechanism for the grassroots use case" framing treats v4 as a deliverable; §10's framing is that the project reaches v4 only if Phase D sustainability materializes, which it may not.
- **Impact:** A funder reading §7.1 sees a self-sustaining product trajectory; §10 names this as an aspiration the brief does not promise (§10.8 line 1168: _"That the project reaches Phase C or D. Reaching them depends on… external funding decisions the project does not control."_). §7's roadmap-as-plan framing is not consistent with §10.8's roadmap-as-conditional-aspiration framing.
- **Recommendation:** Add to §7.1 v4 explicit conditional framing ("v4 and beyond is reached only if Phases C and D sustainably close per §10.3, §10.4, §10.7") and cross-reference §10.8 line 1168's enumeration of what the brief explicitly does not promise.

## Minor findings

### F7: §7.2 "Dependencies between releases" subsection title implies all release dependencies are architectural

- **Evidence:** §7.2 line 678 titled "Dependencies between releases" enumerates only architectural dependencies (v1 enables v1.5, v1.5 enables v2, v2 enables v3). Funding, audit, and governance dependencies — which §8.5 and §10.3 establish as equally hard gates — are not under this heading. A reader looking for "what gates v1.5 release?" finds only architectural answers in §7.2.
- **Impact:** Section-title fidelity matters for skimming readers. §7.2's title promises completeness in dependency enumeration; the subsection delivers only one dimension of dependencies.
- **Recommendation:** Rename §7.2 to "Architectural dependencies between releases" or add a §7.3 "Operational dependencies between releases" naming the funding, audit, and incorporation gates that §§8.4, 8.5, 10 establish.

### F8: §7.1 v3 timing (~18–24 months post-v1) collides with foundation incorporation timing (~18–24 months post-v1) without acknowledgment

- **Evidence:** §7.1 line 674 v3 timing is "~18-24 months post-v1." §8.4 line 766 foundation incorporation timing is "approximately 18-24 months post-v1 launch." The two timelines overlap exactly. The implication — that v3 release and foundation incorporation are concurrent work streams competing for the same developer and same funding pool — is not surfaced in §7.
- **Impact:** A funder modeling whether the project can deliver both v3 and foundation incorporation on the stated timelines does not see the resource collision named. §10.3 / §10.4 Phase D figures (excluding maintainer compensation) at $90K–250K/year suggest one stream is fundable; concurrent execution of both at the stated quality is not modeled.
- **Recommendation:** Acknowledge in §7.1 v3 that the timeline assumes foundation incorporation per §8.4 is sufficiently advanced to absorb operational governance work, or restate v3 timing as conditional on the post-incorporation steady-state operating posture §10.4 describes.

## Patterns

**P1: §§6/7 elide non-architectural gates as a category.** F1, F2, F3, F4, F7 share a structural pattern: §§6 and 7 enumerate architectural commitments and architectural dependencies thoroughly (the forward-compatibility analysis in §6.4 and the inter-version dependency analysis in §7.2 are detailed), while the funding, audit, and governance gates that §§8.4, 8.5, 10 establish as equally hard are not surfaced. A reader who reads §§6/7 first (the natural skim path for "what is v1?" and "what comes next?") forms a model in which the trajectory is governed only by architecture. The brief's actual governance model — architecture _and_ funding _and_ audit _and_ incorporation, each independently gating — is recoverable only by cross-reading §§8 and 10.

**P2: §6.3 pilot framing reads as descriptive when the pilot scope is materially conditional.** F1, F5 share a pattern: §6.3 describes the pilot in terms of what it delivers (10–15 users, 6 months, in-person ceremony, BYOD hardware) without naming what conditions must close before the pilot itself begins (Phase B funding per §10.2) and what conditions must close before pilot exits to broader release (Phase C funding, pre-beta audit, foundation incorporation work). §6.3's tone of operational description is not consistent with §§8.5, 10.2, 10.7's conditional framings.

**P3: §7 roadmap timeline language ("target ~X months") implies project-controlled scheduling.** F2, F6, F8 share a pattern: §7's "target ~6–9 months post-v1" framing reads as a planned schedule. §10.8 line 1167 explicitly states _"Specific timelines tied to funding events. The phase descriptions are 'what unlocks what,' not 'by when.'"_ The honest framing §10.8 commits to is not propagated into §7.1's release-timing language. Funders parsing §7.1 timelines acquire a stronger commitment than §10.8 disclaims.
