# §1 — Honest Non-Promises Lens

## Summary

§1's "What this brief is and is not" paragraph (line 32) attempts to be the high-altitude non-promise set, but it under-echoes §10.8 on three material disclaimers (gross-of-fiscal-sponsor-fees; BYOD hardware-channel availability; the §9.1-to-§10 runway gap, which §1 mentions but mis-frames as a missing-figure rather than a §9.1-commitment breach the funder must account for). §1 also makes several earlier-paragraph claims — Phase B/C/D dollar ranges, "approximately 9–12 person-months" scope, the 18–24-month foundation timeline, the v1.6/v2/v3/v4+ roadmap layering, and a "low hundreds globally" population estimate — that the non-promise paragraph then either does not disclaim or disclaims at a sufficiently abstract altitude that a busy reader will treat them as committed.

The deeper pattern: §1 conflates three distinct framings — committed, intent-subject-to-conditions, and aspirational — under the umbrella terms "phase-gated" and "conditional." §8 and §10 distinguish these carefully; §1 collapses them. A sophisticated funder reading §1 alone will form expectations about subsidy-program availability, fiscal-sponsor netting, runway, and roadmap timing that the deeper sections then walk back. That is exactly the friction §10.8 was written to prevent at the section level, and §1's compression breaks it at the summary level.

## Critical findings

### F1: Gross-of-fiscal-sponsor-fees disclaimer absent from §1

- **PROBLEM:** §1's Phase A–D dollar ranges (line 30: "~$1.5–3.5K… ~$20–60K… ~$105–405K… ~$90–250K/year") are stated without §10.8's explicit warning that Phase B–D figures are gross of fiscal-sponsor fees of 5–15% (often 10–15% at established sponsors).
- **EVIDENCE:** §10.8 line 1247: "That Phase B-D figures are net to the project. The figures are stated gross of fiscal-sponsor fees (5–15% per Section 10.2, often 10–15% at established sponsors)." §1 line 30 reproduces the ranges and §1 line 32's non-promise paragraph omits the netting caveat entirely.
- **IMPACT:** A program officer reading §1 alone takes "~$20–60K addressable through OTF Secure Audit" as the project's working budget envelope; the real planning figure is 85–95% of that during the pre-incorporation window. The understated funding ask is a real number that materially affects "does this project's stated $X cover its stated audit at $Y" pencil-test reasoning at first read. A sophisticated funder who later finds the netting disclaimer in §10.8 will conclude §1 was optimistic to the point of marketing.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add to §1's non-promise paragraph: "that the Phase B–D dollar ranges stated above are net to the project — they are gross of fiscal-sponsor fees per §10.8." One clause. The figure cannot be silently re-baselined in §10.8 if it has already anchored a funder's expectation in §1.

### F2: BYOD hardware-channel-availability disclaimer absent from §1

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 24 names "users without access to GrapheneOS-Pixel hardware through standard commercial channels" as out of scope, but does not flag that even for in-scope users, hardware availability is not consistent across jurisdictions and the project's loaner pool (2–4 devices) does not mitigate jurisdictional channel closures.
- **EVIDENCE:** §10.8 line 1248: "That hardware availability for the BYOD pilot model will be consistent across all candidate-user jurisdictions… the project's small loaner pool (2–4 devices) is the operational mitigation for short-term availability gaps but not for jurisdictional channel closures." §1's audience paragraph (line 24) treats hardware-availability as a binary in/out-of-scope question, masking the gradient §10.8 acknowledges.
- **IMPACT:** A partner organization in a jurisdiction where Pixel availability is unstable (sanctions environments; gray-market device-import regimes; jurisdictions where Google Play Services certification practices affect Pixel imports) reads §1 and treats their constituency as in-scope; learns at §10.8 that the BYOD model assumes channel availability they may not have. This is a partnership-conversation hazard the §1 audience description does not flag.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add to §1's non-promise paragraph: "that GrapheneOS-Pixel hardware will be available through standard commercial channels in every candidate-user jurisdiction — the BYOD posture per §10.1 depends on channel availability the project does not control and the small loaner pool does not address closures at jurisdictional scale."

### F3: Runway gap mis-framed as missing figure rather than as §9.1 commitment breach

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 32 acknowledges "the self-funding runway commitment Section 9.1 makes… is not delivered in this draft and is acknowledged as a §9.1-to-§10 gap in Section 10.8." This treats the gap as a missing data point. §10.8 frames it more sharply as a breached cross-section commitment: §9.1 explicitly stated §10 would deliver this figure as "the financial floor of all v1 funding-related risk discussions." §1's softening loses the discipline §10.8 is enforcing.
- **EVIDENCE:** §10.8 line 1249: "Funders evaluating Phase A duration risk and Phase B funding-window timing should request the runway figure directly from the developer; the brief does not currently commit a number, and Section 10.7's 'All Phase B funding fails for an extended period' failure mode is therefore bounded against an unstated rather than a stated horizon." §1's reference omits the "request directly from the developer" instruction and the bound-against-unstated-horizon framing.
- **IMPACT:** A funder reading only §1 sees an acknowledged gap and infers the project is working on it. A funder reading §10.8 sees an instruction to request the figure and a flagged structural weakness in §10.7's risk analysis. §1's compression converts a known-actionable disclosure into a vague known-incomplete disclosure, downgrading the discipline §9.1 and §10.8 jointly enforce.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Either match §10.8's framing in §1 ("funders should request the v1 self-funding runway figure directly from the developer; §10.7's Phase B funding-failure analysis is bounded against an unstated horizon") or trim the §1 reference to "see §10.8 for the §9.1-to-§10 runway disclosure gap" rather than half-restating it.

## Significant findings

### F4: "Approximately 9–12 person-months for v1" stated without conditions disclaimer

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 28 commits to "approximately 9–12 person-months for v1" engineering scope without echoing that this is a developer estimate that has not been validated against actual implementation progress, and without flagging the wide multiplier between "as available" volunteer cadence and full-time grant-funded cadence.
- **EVIDENCE:** §1 line 28 itself caveats with "calendar-time-to-v1 depends on whether the developer is operating at 'as available' volunteer cadence… or whether grant funding has freed full-time engineering capacity." But the 9–12 person-month figure itself is not disclaimed as an estimate. §9.1's "Brief-to-funded-development timeline may slip" risk treats the timeline as conditional but does not re-baseline the person-month estimate.
- **IMPACT:** Person-month estimates from solo developers on novel cryptographic-integration work routinely run 2–3× over. A funder calibrating "what does $X buy" against 9–12 person-months will mis-size the ask. The non-promise paragraph does not flag this.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add to §1's non-promise paragraph: "that the 9–12 person-month v1 engineering estimate will hold once implementation begins — the estimate is the developer's working figure and is subject to discovery during implementation." Alternatively, qualify the §1 number itself with "estimated."

### F5: Foundation-incorporation timeline stated as "approximately 18–24 months post-v1" without explicit aspirational framing

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 30 states "Foundation incorporation is aspirational at approximately 18–24 months post-v1 per D0010 (placeholder pending legal consultation)." The word "aspirational" appears but is immediately followed by a concrete timeframe and a placeholder marker, conflating three framings (intent, dependency, placeholder).
- **EVIDENCE:** §10.8 line 1245 disclaims jurisdiction of incorporation as an open question (Q4). §8.4 frames the foundation path as gated on Phase C funding and partner conversations. §1's "aspirational at 18–24 months" reads as a soft date in a brief whose §10.8 framing is "what unlocks what, not when."
- **IMPACT:** §1's 18–24-month figure becomes the timeline cited in the §1 reader's working memory, even though §10.8's "what unlocks what, not when" discipline would have it stated as conditional on Phase C close, partner conversations, and legal consultation. The "aspirational" hedge is too weak to undo the anchor a number creates.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Either drop the "approximately 18–24 months post-v1" in §1 entirely (refer the reader to §8.4) or add to the non-promise paragraph: "that foundation incorporation occurs on the 18–24-month post-v1 horizon stated above — the horizon is the project's working aspiration, gated on Phase C funding closing and on legal-jurisdiction consultation (Q4)."

### F6: v1.5/v1.6/v2/v3/v4+ roadmap layering disclaimed only at v2/v3 altitude

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 32's non-promise paragraph disclaims "v2 iOS and v3 mesh-radio extensions will materialize on any specific timeline" but does not extend the same discipline to v1.5 (Briar tier; reproducible Android builds) or v1.6 (multi-profile UX; in-app post-coercion recovery; duress-wipe; possible localization). §1 line 28 layered v1.5 and v1.6 with the same phase-gate language; the non-promise paragraph only disclaims v2/v3.
- **EVIDENCE:** §1 line 28: "Subsequent releases (v1.5 architecture completeness; v1.6 deferred UX; v2 USB and iOS; v3 mesh radio; v4+ established-organization tier) are described with phase-gate language consistent with Section 10.1's 'as available' volunteer-baseline cadence." Line 32 then disclaims only v2/v3 by name. v1.5 is structurally gated on Phase C funding per §1 itself; v1.6 inherits the same gating; neither is named in the non-promise paragraph.
- **IMPACT:** A reader who sees v2/v3 disclaimed but not v1.5/v1.6 infers v1.5 and v1.6 are nearer-term commitments. They are not — §1 line 28 says v1.5 broader-than-pilot release is structurally gated on Phase C funding. The selective disclaim creates a false near-term/far-term distinction.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Generalize the non-promise: "that v1.5, v1.6, v2 iOS, v3 mesh-radio, or v4+ established-organization extensions will materialize on any specific timeline — the phase-gate descriptions are 'what unlocks what,' not 'by when.'" One sentence covers the whole roadmap layer.

### F7: "Named subsidy programs will be open at the rates cited" disclaim repeated but not landed against the §1 figures

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 32 disclaims "that the named subsidy programs will be open at the rates cited when Cairn applies" — but the §1 figures (Phase B "~$20–60K addressable through OTF Secure Audit, NLnet NGI Zero Trust, or Cure53 mission-rate engagement"; Phase C "~$105–405K depending on audit firm and UX engineer engagement") were stated against the rates §10.8 says will require re-baselining.
- **EVIDENCE:** §10.8 line 1246: "Subsidy-program landscape and audit-firm rate cards both shift on multi-year horizons (OTF's funding was contested in 2024-2025; Mozilla restructured its open-source funding repeatedly; security-engineering market rates moved materially during 2020-2025). Floors and ceilings are stated against the landscape as of brief publication and require re-baselining at each application cycle." §1 has the disclaim but not the "stated against landscape as of brief publication" framing — the figures themselves are not timestamped.
- **IMPACT:** A funder evaluating the brief six months after publication takes the §1 ranges at face value; the program landscape (especially OTF's funding contestation) may have shifted materially. The §1 disclaim mentions the program may not be open but does not mention the rates themselves are landscape-dated.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add a parenthetical to §1's figure presentation: "Phase B ~$20–60K (rates and subsidy-program availability as of brief publication, per §10.8 re-baselining caveat)." Or extend the non-promise paragraph: "the dollar ranges above are stated against the subsidy and rate-card landscape as of brief publication and require re-baselining at each application cycle per §10.8."

## Minor findings

### F8: "Low hundreds globally" population estimate not disclaimed as an estimate the project commits to refine

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 24 commits to "the estimated population for the full four-precondition v1 intersection is on the order of low hundreds globally (Q17)." The Q17 reference flags it as open but §1's non-promise paragraph does not echo the structural uncertainty.
- **EVIDENCE:** §2.2 line 69 explicitly frames this as "an estimated population size for the four-precondition v1 intersection… Q17 in open-questions.md commits the project to refining this estimate through partner-organization outreach." §1 reproduces the figure as a parenthetical without the refinement-commitment framing.
- **IMPACT:** A program officer evaluating the project's market sizing takes "low hundreds globally" as the project's analytical assessment; it is the developer's pre-outreach working estimate that the project explicitly intends to refine. Difference matters for grant evaluation.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add "(working estimate pending partner-outreach refinement per Q17)" rather than the bare "(Q17)" in §1 line 24, so the reader sees the estimate's epistemic status without having to chase to open-questions.md.

### F9: "First-quorum reviewer attestation forming" gates v1 pilot, but §1 does not disclaim that the quorum may not form

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 28 states "pilot deployment is gated on… first-quorum reviewer attestation forming per Section 8.2." This is correct, but the brief does not disclaim that the quorum may not form within any specific window. §8.2 frames reviewer-pool recruitment as conditional on Q3 (honoraria funding) and Q5 (NGO outreach). The gate is real; the silence about whether it closes is louder than the disclaimer about timeline.
- **EVIDENCE:** §1 line 30: "Reviewer-pool recruitment, partner-organization arrangements, and witness-pool composition are conditional on Q3 (funding for honoraria) and Q5 (NGO partner outreach) per Section 8.6." This is the conditionality framing but it does not state the structural risk that the quorum might never form — only that recruitment is conditional.
- **IMPACT:** The reader sees "conditional on Q3 and Q5" and assumes those are tractable. The brief's §9.1 "Reviewer and witness pools may not form or may erode" risk is a different and more severe framing the §1 compression elides.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Either reference §9.1 explicitly in §1's conditional-framing sentence ("conditional on Q3 and Q5 per §8.6; pool-non-formation risk per §9.1") or add to the non-promise paragraph: "that the reviewer and witness pools form within any specific window — pool formation is structurally conditional on Q3 funding and Q5 outreach per §8.6, with non-formation risk surfaced in §9.1."

### F10: Researcher Safe Harbor framing under-qualified in §1

- **PROBLEM:** §1 line 30 says "researcher Safe Harbor is the project's stated intent until incorporation, with formalization through a standard template at incorporation per D0012." This is accurate but elides §8.5's sharper framing: "this is a published preference rather than a legal protection — the project is operated by a natural person (the developer) whose commitment is not enforceable against future personal action, successors, or coercion-induced exceptions."
- **EVIDENCE:** §8.5 line 874. §1 line 30 takes the milder "stated intent" framing.
- **IMPACT:** A security researcher considering disclosure to v1 Cairn reads §1 and treats Safe Harbor as a stated-intent matter to weigh; the actual posture is "not enforceable" by structure. The §1 framing is materially weaker than §8.5's.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add to §1: "(unenforceable against future personal action, successors, or coercion-induced exceptions per §8.5 until foundation incorporation)" — or relocate to non-promise paragraph: "researcher Safe Harbor is not legally enforceable until foundation incorporation per §8.5."

### F11: "If partner outreach succeeds" dependency packed into qualifying phrases without external visibility

- **PROBLEM:** §1 references partner outreach (Q5) in multiple places — reviewer-pool recruitment, partnership arrangements, broader-deployment facilitation — but does not surface to the reader that Q5 is one external dependency funneling into many separate downstream commitments. §1 line 30 lists "partner outreach" as a condition for one item; §1 line 28 makes v1.5 broader release conditional on Phase C funding; the reader has to chain the references to see that partner outreach gates multiple paths.
- **EVIDENCE:** §1 lines 28 and 30 each reference partner outreach in different contexts. §2.3's caveat (line 87) treats Q5 as the falsifier on the differentiation argument. §8.6 frames partner conversations as not yet held. The cumulative dependency on Q5 is dispersed.
- **IMPACT:** Sophisticated reader recognizes the dispersion as obscuring a concentrated dependency. A single failed phase of partner outreach affects reviewer pool, deployment facilitation, differentiation argument, and direct-grant credibility (per §10.9). §1 does not make this concentration visible.
- **RECOMMENDATION:** Add to §1 (either the operational paragraph or the non-promise paragraph): "Q5 partner-organization outreach is a concentrated dependency: it gates reviewer-pool recruitment, broader-deployment facilitation, differentiation-argument validation per Q18, and direct-grant credibility per §10.9. The brief's confidence in the project's broader trajectory is contingent on outreach the project has not yet conducted." This is the silence a sophisticated reader will notice.

## Patterns

- **Pattern 1 — Selective disclaim by altitude.** §1's non-promise paragraph disclaims at the funding-event and roadmap-extension altitudes (v2/v3, dollar amounts, audience-not-served-at-v1) but does not disclaim at the operating-figures altitude (rates, fees, person-months, runway, population estimates). §10.8 disclaims at both altitudes. §1's compression preserves the high-altitude discipline and loses the operating-figure discipline. Funders read the operating figures.

- **Pattern 2 — Conflation of three framings.** "Committed," "intent subject to conditions," and "aspirational" are distinct in §8/§10 but collapsed in §1 under "conditional" and "phase-gated." The §1 non-promise paragraph's instruction to "treat Phase A items as the project's unilateral commitments, all later phases as conditional" is correct but coarse. §10.8's discipline includes distinguishing aspirational maintainer-compensation from intent-subject-to-conditions reviewer-honoraria; §1 does not preserve that gradient.

- **Pattern 3 — Cross-section commitment breaches mentioned but not flagged.** §1 acknowledges the §9.1-to-§10 runway gap (F3) but frames it as a missing figure rather than a documented breached commitment. The pattern would extend to any other cross-section commitment that gets walked back — §1's non-promise paragraph would benefit from a category for "where this brief breaches a commitment another section made and the breach is named honestly."

- **Pattern 4 — Anchor figures stated cleanly, hedge figures hedged late.** §1's $1.5–3.5K Phase A, $20–60K Phase B, $105–405K Phase C, $90–250K/year Phase D, 9–12 person-months v1, 18–24-month foundation, "low hundreds globally" v1 population, and 10–15 pilot users are all stated as bare figures in the body and hedged in the non-promise paragraph at varying altitudes (or not at all). The discipline §10.8 enforces — landscape-dated, gross-of-fees, request-runway-directly, hardware-channel-conditional — does not propagate cleanly upward.

- **Pattern 5 — Honest non-promises §1 could profitably add by silence-detection.** Sophisticated readers will notice the following silences: (a) no statement of what the project does if the pilot succeeds operationally but the audit funding does not close before pilot completion that is sharper than §8.5's "defer broader release"; (b) no statement of who decides the project sunsets if §9.4 conditions trigger and the developer is the sole decision-maker; (c) no statement of what "informed by feedback on this document" means operationally — does feedback that reframes the project's vehicle question (Q18 → contributing to Briar/SimpleX) trigger a documented re-scoping protocol or a developer judgment call; (d) no statement of whether the brief itself is versioned and how readers know which version a partner conversation referenced. These are silences §1 could break with single-clause non-promises and gain credibility.
