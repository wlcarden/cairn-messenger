# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Cairn is committed to providing a welcoming, harassment-free experience for
everyone who takes part in the project, regardless of background or personal
characteristics. Contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and community members
are expected to act professionally and treat one another with respect.

## Standard adopted

This project adopts the **Contributor Covenant, version 2.1** as its code of
conduct. The authoritative, complete document — the behavioral standards, the
enforcement responsibilities, scope, and the tiered enforcement guidelines —
is the canonical text published here:

- Full code of conduct:
  https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/
- Enforcement guidelines:
  https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines

By participating in this project (contributing code, documentation, issues,
discussions, or reviews) you agree to uphold that standard. The summary below
is a convenience; the linked Contributor Covenant text governs in full.

## Expected behavior

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project's users

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment of any kind, whether in public or private project spaces
- Demeaning, discriminatory, or derogatory comments; personal or political
  attacks
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email
  address) without their explicit permission
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

The authoritative and complete enumeration is in the Contributor Covenant
text linked above.

## Reporting

Report concerns to the project maintainer at **conduct@cairn-project.org**
(placeholder; the operational address and its PGP key will be published
before v1 alpha).

For **security vulnerabilities**, do **not** use this address — use the
separate disclosure channel in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) instead.

All reports are reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer
is obligated to respect the privacy and security of the person who reports an
incident.

## Enforcement

At the project's current solo-maintainer stage, the maintainer is responsible
for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct and may take any action
deemed appropriate — up to and including a temporary or permanent ban from
project spaces — following the Contributor Covenant's tiered enforcement
guidelines (Correction → Warning → Temporary Ban → Permanent Ban) linked
above.

Conflicts at the architectural-decision layer are handled through the
project's decision-record (`docs/decisions/`) and open-questions
(`docs/open-questions.md`) process, not through this policy; this policy
governs conduct in community spaces.
